AMR Summary

Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) occurs when bacteria, fungi, unicellular and multicellular organisms fail to respond to medicines, making infections harder to treat.

AMR is one of the top 10 global public health threats facing humanity. It can affect anyone, of any age, in any country. Without effective antimicrobials, modern medicine is at risk.

Our organization works tirelessly through research, education, policy advocacy, and community engagement to preserve these life-saving medicines for future generations.

Our Mission
